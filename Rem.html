Rem is just an adorable little familiar who waddles about with an army of crowlike minions at his side.
Rem's Collection
Pillow Toss
Rem throws a pillow that damages and knocks back enemies. What makes it more than just a poke tool is the passive mechanic baked in — landing hits reduces the cooldown of Rem's other abilities, meaning every accurate toss accelerates his whole kit. It's best used as a counter-engage, with the knockback capable of stopping channeled abilities or creating separation from aggressive frontliners. Simple on paper, but keeping it on cooldown and landing it consistently is what separates good Rem players from great ones.

Tag Along
Rem targets an ally hero, dashes over to them, falls asleep on their shoulder, and heals both himself and the ally — with the heal scaling off both characters' missing health, followed by a lingering regeneration. He can reactivate the ability to hop to a separate ally and refresh it on the new target, once per ally — and while attached, he's largely untargetable unless hit by an ultimate with a stun. The result is an ability that's simultaneously a heal, an escape tool, and a way to make Rem almost impossible to focus down in a chaotic teamfight.

Lil helpers
they can collect boxes, perform Sinner's Sacrifices, follow allies to provide healing and buffs, pick up souls, and even be sent to shops to Quick Buy items on Rem's behalf. It's the quirkiest ability in the game by a wide margin, and also deceptively powerful. Helpers can steal enemy Sinner's Sacrifices from across the map and snag Bridge Buffs from a distance, giving Rem a level of map-wide economic disruption that no other hero can match. Easy to overlook, impossible to replace once you understand the value.

Naptime
Rem's eyes project a gaze forward that pierces through walls and floors, slowing any enemies caught inside it, reducing their dash distance, and disabling their movement abilities entirely. When the channel ends, caught enemies fall asleep — and when they wake up, they take a burst of heavy spirit damage. With fantastic range and the ability to look through terrain, it's a phenomenal teamfight ultimate capable of singling out mobile heroes or setting up full team engages. The sleep effect means enemies have to choose between escaping before it resolves or eating the damage — often they can't do either.
